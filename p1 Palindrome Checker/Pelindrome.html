            What is a palindrome?

            A palindrome is a word or phrase that can be read the same way backward or forward, as with the words redivider,
            kayak, and civic.

            Palindromes are most often used to be creative and have fun with words. You might see them in ads to catch your
            attention or a creative writing teacher might ask the class to write palindromes to help students think more
            creatively.

            In biology, a palindrome describes a sequence of nucleotides in DNA that matches a reverse line. The possible
            nucleotide combinations are A-T and C-G, and the pairs can match up in any order. Sometimes they line up
            palindromically, as when CCTAGG matches up with a sequence of GGATCC.

            Example: One of the most famous palindromes is “Never Odd or Even,” which has the same letters backward and
            forward.
